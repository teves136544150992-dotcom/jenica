Life on Land

"Life on Land" focuses on the protection, restoration, and sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems â€” forests, wetlands, drylands, and mountains. Healthy land sustains biodiversity, supports agriculture, stores carbon, and provides water and livelihoods for millions.

Why it matters

Terrestrial ecosystems provide essential services: pollination, soil fertility, water regulation, and climate moderation. Conserving land helps fight climate change and preserves species and cultural heritage.

Main threats

  • Deforestation and habitat loss
  • Overexploitation of natural resources
  • Invasive species and biodiversity decline
  • Climate change and desertification

Conservation actions

Practical steps include protecting remaining natural habitats, restoring degraded land, promoting sustainable agriculture, and supporting community-based conservation. Policies, local leadership, and finance are all needed to scale impact.

Examples

Reforestation projects, protected area expansion, agroforestry, and invasive-species control have all shown positive results when designed with local communities.

How you can help

  1. Support organizations working on land restoration.
  2. Choose sustainable products (timber, palm oil, paper).
  3. Participate in local tree-planting and clean-up events.
  4. Reduce food waste and choose diverse diets to lower pressure on land.
